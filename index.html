ABOUT THE SIMULATION

Iron Dome: Defense OS is a tactical defense simulation that combines strategy, reflexes, and precision targeting.

Players operate a military-style command interface responsible for intercepting incoming threats before they reach protected cities. The system must constantly track hostile projectiles while launching interceptors at precisely calculated moments.

It's all about timing and not wasting your shots. You can't just spam interceptors because you've got limited ammo, your systems need time to reload, and your radar can only track so much at once. Success depends on maintaining control under pressure while coordinating the defense grid.

DEFENSE GRID OPERATIONS

At the center of the game is the defensive interception grid.

Incoming threats appear on the radar as missiles approach from different directions. Players must quickly identify targets and deploy interceptors before they reach the ground.

The grid constantly processes new threats, requiring players to prioritize targets and protect vulnerable areas.

If too many missiles pass through the defense system, the cities under protection will be destroyed.

FAQ

  • The main objective is to defend a group of cities from incoming missile attacks. Players must intercept hostile projectiles before they reach the ground. Each successful interception protects the cities and earns credits for upgrades. If all cities are destroyed, the mission ends.

  • Players tap on incoming missiles to launch interceptors. The system calculates the trajectory and detonates the interceptor at the correct point in mid-air. Proper timing is essential because missiles travel at different speeds and angles. Accurate targeting prevents them from reaching the cities below.

  • The defense grid protects six cities. Every missile that successfully reaches the ground destroys part of the city infrastructure. Once all cities are lost, the defense mission fails. The challenge is to maintain the defense grid for as long as possible.

  • Yes, players earn credits during successful defense operations. These credits can be used to upgrade the defense system. Improvements include faster reload systems, expanded interceptor batteries, and enhanced explosive interceptors. These upgrades help manage heavier attack waves.

  • Yes, the game features an escalating threat system. Each wave introduces more missiles with greater speed and complexity. Players must react faster and use resources more efficiently. This escalation ensures the gameplay remains challenging and dynamic.
